Distracted Driver Detection

There was a competition on kaggle about detecting distracted drivers from dashcam snapshot photos of drivers. The training dataset consisted of 26 drivers and 10 classes for a total of 22424 images. The task was to classify each image into one of the 10 classes as such “Holding cellphone on the right hand” or “Reaching for backseat”. I experimented with numerous state of the art deep convolutional neural networks such as Resnet, VGG-CAM, VGG16 and GoogleNet. Eventually, I used a mixture of 13 fine-tuned VGG16 CNNs implemented in Python using Keras with Theano backend, plus addition code for various tasks along the pipline such as data cleaning and visualization.

NOAA Storm Data

We are interested in answering two questions, (1) Across the United States, which types of events are most harmful with respect to population health? and (2) which types of events have the greatest economic consequences? We use the storm data published by NOAA to find the total cost in USD and total casualties per event type to determine the top five event types that are most harmful in terms of health and in terms of economy.
